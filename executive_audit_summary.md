# Executive Audit Summary

## Project
**Local SEO Audit and Fix Plan**  
**Tools:** Screaming Frog, Google Search Console  
**Business Type:** Home services website

## Executive Summary
This project focused on auditing a local home services website to identify the technical and on-page SEO issues most likely to suppress service page visibility, crawl efficiency, and local lead generation performance. Using Screaming Frog and Google Search Console together, the audit was structured to evaluate crawlability, indexation, metadata quality, internal linking, duplicate content risk, and service page alignment with local search intent.

The review surfaced several issues commonly found on small to mid-sized local business websites, including inconsistent indexation across key service pages, duplicate or weak metadata, overlapping page intent, and under-supported internal linking to high-value money pages. Search Console analysis also highlighted opportunities to improve click-through rate on pages already earning impressions, as well as page-query mismatches that suggested unclear targeting or thin on-page relevance.

The resulting action plan prioritised fixes based on SEO impact, business impact, and implementation effort. Highest-priority recommendations focused on improving indexation signals, strengthening service page uniqueness, tightening title tag and heading structure, and increasing internal links to core lead-driving pages. Supporting recommendations included improving local relevance signals, clarifying page intent, and building a lightweight monitoring routine to track changes in visibility and crawl health over time.

## Audit Objectives
- Identify technical SEO issues affecting crawlability and indexation
- Improve service page visibility for local-intent searches
- Strengthen internal linking to core revenue-generating pages
- Reduce duplication and page overlap across service content
- Create a practical fix roadmap tied to local lead generation goals

## Core Audit Areas
### Screaming Frog Review
- Status codes and crawl depth
- Indexability and noindex issues
- Canonical consistency
- Redirects and redirect chains
- Duplicate titles and meta descriptions
- Heading structure and content gaps
- Internal linking distribution and orphan page risk
- Image alt text and oversized asset observations where relevant

### Google Search Console Review
- Performance report analysis
- Low CTR, high-impression page opportunities
- Page and query mapping alignment
- Coverage and indexation review
- Priority page identification based on impressions, clicks, CTR, and position

### Local SEO Review
- Service page targeting and uniqueness
- Local modifiers and geographic relevance
- Internal links to money pages
- CTA clarity and page conversion support
- Duplicate or overlapping service and location intent

## Example Findings
**Note:** The following findings are demo assumptions for portfolio presentation purposes.

| Finding | Why It Matters | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Key service pages had duplicate or weak title tags | Limits differentiation in search results and can reduce CTR | High |
| Some intended money pages were not strongly supported by internal links | Makes it harder for search engines to understand page importance | High |
| GSC showed high impressions but low CTR on selected service pages | Indicates snippet and intent alignment opportunities | High |
| Multiple pages appeared to target overlapping service intent | Creates cannibalization risk and weakens relevance signals | Medium |
| A small set of pages showed indexation inconsistency or weak canonical signals | Can reduce visibility for pages that should rank locally | High |

## Prioritisation Logic
Recommendations were prioritised using a practical framework:
- **High impact / low effort:** implement first
- **High impact / medium effort:** schedule in core roadmap
- **Medium impact / low effort:** complete after core fixes
- **Low impact:** defer unless needed

## Recommended Priority Actions
1. Rewrite and standardise title tags and meta descriptions for core service pages
2. Improve internal linking to high-value service pages from navigation, related content, and contextual sections
3. Review indexability and canonical implementation for intended ranking pages
4. Consolidate or differentiate overlapping service pages
5. Strengthen local relevance with unique service copy, trust signals, and location context
6. Monitor GSC CTR and position shifts after updates

## Monitoring Plan
### Weekly
- Clicks
- Impressions
- CTR
- Average position
- Page-level movement for priority service pages
- Coverage alerts or indexation anomalies if relevant

### Monthly
- Re-crawl key sections in Screaming Frog
- Validate fixes implemented
- Compare visibility trends across money pages
- Review internal linking improvements
- Identify new optimisation opportunities from GSC data

## 30-60-90 Day Roadmap
### First 30 Days
- Fix metadata issues on priority pages
- Review indexation blockers
- Clean up canonicals and redirects
- Improve core internal linking

### Days 31-60
- Refine service page copy for uniqueness and local relevance
- Address overlapping page intent
- Improve trust signals and conversion elements
- Reassess query-page alignment in GSC

### Days 61-90
- Measure movement in impressions, CTR, and positions
- Re-crawl priority sections
- Expand optimisation to secondary pages
- Refresh backlog based on new performance data

## Expected Outcome
This audit and fix plan was designed to improve crawl health, strengthen local service page visibility, and create a more structured path from technical SEO remediation to local lead generation growth.

## Portfolio Positioning
Completed a local SEO audit using Screaming Frog and Google Search Console to identify indexation, internal linking, duplicate content, and service page optimisation opportunities, then built a prioritised fix roadmap and monitoring plan for a home services business.
