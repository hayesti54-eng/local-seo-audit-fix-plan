# Local SEO Audit and Fix Plan

### Screaming Frog | Real Business Audit | HHH Roofing & Construction

---

## Overview

This project documents a real local SEO audit completed for **HHH Roofing & Construction**, a Houston-based roofing company with residential, commercial, and location-focused pages.

The audit used **Screaming Frog** and manual page review to evaluate crawl health, metadata quality, heading structure, canonical handling, internal linking, and local service-page intent. The goal was to identify the technical and on-page SEO issues most likely to limit local search visibility and lead generation performance.

Unlike a generic template audit, this project was built around a live business website and real crawl findings.

## Real Crawl Findings

A live Screaming Frog crawl of the HHH Roofing Houston section surfaced a focused but meaningful set of technical and on-page SEO issues.

### Technical Findings
- **12 redirects** identified across a relatively small crawl set
- **1 client error (404)** identified
- a legacy commercial/local-intent URL, `/houston-tpo-roofer`, returned **404 Not Found**
- `/tpo-roof/houston/` returned a **301 redirect**
- at least one page appeared **canonicalised and non-indexable**, requiring validation

### Metadata Findings
- **10 page titles** exceeded recommended display length
- **2 page titles** were too short
- **11 meta descriptions** exceeded recommended character length
- **9 meta descriptions** exceeded pixel-width best practice

### Heading Findings
- **4 pages** were missing H1 tags
- **3 pages** showed non-sequential heading structure

### Internal Linking Findings
- crawl depth remained relatively shallow, which is positive
- however, many internal links appeared to rely on weak or non-descriptive anchor implementation
- priority service pages may not be receiving enough contextual internal-link support

---

## Manual Page Review Findings

In addition to the crawl, key live pages were reviewed manually.

### What Was Working
- strong local modifiers on the Houston residential page
- clear conversion actions including phone and inspection CTAs
- visible trust elements such as licensed and insured messaging
- strong residential and commercial service segmentation
- local neighborhood references that support Houston relevance

### Key SEO Risks
- broad Houston location intent may overlap with narrower residential or commercial Houston pages
- some redirected legacy URLs may not preserve the strongest service-specific relevance
- commercial roofing pages may need clearer internal-link pathways from broader hub pages
- page targeting should be reviewed to reduce intent overlap between general location pages and narrower service pages

---

## Priority Issues

### High Priority
1. Broken 404 URL at `/houston-tpo-roofer`
2. Redirect cleanup needed across legacy and alternate location/service URLs
3. Overlength title tags on important service and location pages
4. Missing H1 tags on multiple pages
5. Canonical validation needed on at least one non-indexable page
6. Potential mismatch between legacy TPO Houston URL intent and final destination

### Medium Priority
1. Overlength meta descriptions affecting SERP presentation
2. Weak internal-link context to important service pages
3. Possible overlap between broad Houston page intent and narrower service-intent pages
4. Under-optimised short titles on selected pages

---

## Recommended Action Plan

### Phase 1: Technical Cleanup
- resolve the 404 URL with a relevant 301 redirect or page restoration
- update internal links to point directly to final 200-status URLs
- review all 3xx URLs and confirm they resolve to the most relevant destination
- validate canonical handling on any non-indexable canonicalised page

### Phase 2: Metadata and Heading Optimisation
- rewrite overlength title tags for cleaner SERP display
- improve short or under-optimised titles
- shorten meta descriptions for stronger CTR control
- add missing H1s and correct heading hierarchy issues

### Phase 3: Local Page Structure and Intent Alignment
- clarify the role of the broad Houston page versus narrower residential and commercial pages
- strengthen local service targeting without creating internal competition
- improve internal links between hub, service, and location pages
- reinforce contextual anchor text to key money pages

### Phase 4: Monitoring and Iteration
- re-crawl priority sections after fixes
- verify redirect cleanup and error resolution
- monitor changes in title and description quality
- track visibility and engagement improvements in future GSC reporting
